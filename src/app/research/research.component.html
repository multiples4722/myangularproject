Research Objectives and Overall Strategy

The proposed research will address the following four Technical Challenges (TCs) and will provide unique UAM solutions and a transition roadmap for industry/government to utilize resulting products

  • TC1: Safe Perception, Coordination, Planning, and Navigation
  • TC2: Secured Autonomy
  • TC3: Verification & Validation (V&V) and Test & Evaluation (T&E)
  • TC4: System Integration

TC1: Safe Perception, Coordination, Planning, and Navigation

will develop computationally effective and scalable sensing, perception, coordination, planning, and learning-based control techniques, to provide a safe and scalable UAM network.

TC2: Secured Autonomy

will address (Objective I) Mathematical Modeling of UAM and Cyberattack, (Objective II) Cyberattack Analysis, Detection, and Risk Management, (Objective III) AI-driven Cyberattack Monitoring.

TC3: Verification & Validation (V&V) and Test & Evaluation (T&E)

: will develop V&V procedures to provide provable guarantees of correctness of the UAM software and support certification of the developed technologies through T&E. (II) will verify algorithms developed in TC1 and TC2, and provid Verification & Validation formal framework to analyze results of tests in TC4.

TC4: System Integration

plays the critical role of evaluation and system-level experimentation, serving as the centerpiece of the iteration and continuous refinement with TCs 1-3 via: (I) Computational system analysis via M&S, (II) Flight experiments at increasing size and sophistication, (III) interface design with industry transition partners, (IV) Continuous assessment of emerging mission requirements
